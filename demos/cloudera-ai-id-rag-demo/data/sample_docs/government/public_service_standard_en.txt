CHAPTER I — LEGAL BASIS AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

Article 1 — Legal Basis

a. Law No. 25 of 2009 on Public Services
b. Law No. 23 of 2014 on Regional Government
c. Government Regulation No. 96 of 2012 on Implementation of the Public Services Law
d. Ministerial Regulation (Permenpan-RB) No. 15 of 2014 on Service Standards Guidelines
e. Nusantara City Regional Regulation No. 4 of 2023 on Public Service Standards

Article 2 — Definitions

2.1 Service Standard is the benchmark used as a guideline for delivering public services
    and as a reference for evaluating service quality.
2.2 Community Satisfaction Index (IKM) is data and information on the level of community
    satisfaction obtained by quantitatively and qualitatively measuring public opinion
    on the services received.
2.3 Service Pledge (Maklumat Pelayanan) is a written statement from the service provider
    committing to deliver services in accordance with the established standards.
2.4 Complaint is the expression of dissatisfaction arising from actual or anticipated harm
    suffered by a service applicant.

Article 6 — Compensation for Service Delays

If a service exceeds the established time standard, applicants are entitled to:
a. Formal apology from the head of the service unit
b. Priority queue on the next visit
c. Document delivery to the applicant's address (for delays exceeding 2x the standard)
d. Escalation report to the unit head's superior for systemic delays

For IMB/PBG permits specifically:
- Delay of 1–5 days   : 10% discount on PNBP fees
- Delay of 6–14 days  : 25% discount on PNBP fees
- Delay of more than 14 days: 50% discount on PNBP fees + internal investigation

| Service Unit                  | Minimum IKM Target | Minimum Quality Grade |
|------------------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Civil Registration (Disdukcapil)| 82.0 (Good)     | B                     |
| Licensing Office (DPTSP)      | 80.0 (Good)        | B                     |
| Social Services               | 83.0 (Good)        | B                     |
| Sub-district offices          | 81.0 (Good)        | B                     |
| City General Hospital (RSUD)  | 80.0 (Good)        | B                     |
| City average target           | 81.5 (Good)        | B                     |

IKM score to quality grade conversion:
- IKM 88.31 – 100.00 : Grade A (Very Good)
- IKM 76.61 – 88.30  : Grade B (Good)
- IKM 65.00 – 76.60  : Grade C (Poor)
- IKM below 65.00    : Grade D (Very Poor) — mandatory improvement plan required

Article 8 — IKM Measurement

IKM is measured across 9 elements per Permenpan-RB No. 14 of 2017:
1. Requirements   — ease and clarity of required documents
2. Procedure      — ease and clarity of the service flow
3. Time           — adherence to the established service time standard
4. Cost           — compliance with the stipulated fee schedule
5. Product        — whether the service received matched what was expected
6. Competence     — ability of officers in service delivery
7. Behaviour      — courtesy and friendliness of officers
8. Complaint handling — ability to resolve complaints
9. Facilities and infrastructure — comfort of the service environment

CHAPTER IV — COMPLAINT CHANNELS AND HANDLING

Article 9 — Official Complaint Channels

Channels available for citizens to lodge complaints:
a. NusantaraKu App (Android/iOS):
   - First response: within 24 hours
   - Available 24/7, directly integrated with relevant government agencies (SKPD)

b. City Hotline 1500-XXX:
   - Business hours service: Monday–Friday 08:00–16:00 WIB
   - Infrastructure emergencies: 24/7

c. Complaints Counter at City Hall:
   - Operating hours: Monday–Friday 08:00–15:00 WIB
   - Dedicated complaint handling officers

d. Official email: pengaduan@nusantarakota.go.id
   - Initial response: within 2 business days

e. Written submission to the City Ombudsman office:
   - For complaints not resolved at the SKPD level

Article 10 — Complaint Handling Procedure

Stages of complaint handling:
1. Registration and verification : 1 business day
2. Clarification with the relevant unit: 3 business days
3. Investigation and analysis: 5–10 business days (depending on complexity)
4. Response to complainant: no later than 14 business days from receipt
5. Follow-up monitoring: 30 days after the response

Complaint resolution deadlines by category:
- Administrative complaints (documents, processing time): 7 business days
- Officer behaviour complaints: 10 business days
- Policy/regulatory complaints: 21 business days
- Cross-agency complaints: 30 business days

CHAPTER V — CONTROL AND EVALUATION MECHANISMS

Article 11 — Routine Reporting

Service units must submit reports to the Regional Secretary:
- Monthly service performance report (IKM, application volume, on-time rate)
- Monthly complaint report (count, type, resolution status)
- Annual service standard evaluation report

Article 12 — Sanctions for Service Units

Service units that consistently fail to meet standards face:
- First written warning: IKM below target for 2 consecutive months
- Second written warning and 30-day improvement plan: IKM below target for 3 months
- External service audit: IKM below target for 4 months or high complaint volume
- Leadership review: IKM below target for 6 consecutive months
