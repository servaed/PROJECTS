d. Government Regulation No. 12 of 2019 on Regional Finance Management

   Regional Finance Management
f. Nusantara City Regional Regulation No. 1 of 2026 on the FY2026 Budget (APBD)

Article 2 — Definitions

2.1 APBD is the annual financial plan of the regional government established by
    regional regulation (Peraturan Daerah).
2.2 Budget Ceiling (Pagu) is the maximum approved budget allocation for a programme
    or activity within a single fiscal year.
2.3 Budget Realisation is the amount of spending that has actually been incurred and
    accounted for in a given period.
2.4 Budget Absorption Rate is the percentage of realised expenditure against the
    approved budget ceiling.
2.5 Regional Work Unit (SKPD) is a unit of the regional government organisation
    responsible for executing specific programmes and activities.

CHAPTER III — BUDGET CYCLE AND EXECUTION

Article 5 — Budget Cycle Schedule

Standard schedule for FY2026 budget execution:

Planning and Adoption:
- Village-level Musrenbang       : January–February (completed)
- District-level Musrenbang      : February (completed)
- City-level Musrenbang          : March (completed)
- RKPD (Work Plan) adoption      : May 2025 (completed)
- FY2026 APBD adoption           : December 2025 (completed)

Execution:
- Preparation of SKPD Budget Documents (DPA): January 2026 (completed)
- Q1 budget execution            : January–March 2026
- Q1 evaluation                  : April 2026
- Q2 budget execution            : April–June 2026
- Q2 evaluation + Budget revision: July–August 2026
- Q3 execution                   : July–September 2026
- Q4 execution                   : October–December 2026

Accountability:
- Financial statement preparation : January–February 2027
- BPK (Supreme Audit Agency) audit: March–May 2027
- Adoption of LKPD Regional Regulation: July 2027

Article 6 — Quarterly Budget Absorption Targets

Cumulative budget absorption targets set by the Mayor:

| Quarter | Cumulative Absorption Target | Minimum Threshold |
|---------|------------------------------|-------------------|
| Q1      | 20% of ceiling               | 15%               |
| Q2      | 45% of ceiling               | 38%               |
| Q3      | 70% of ceiling               | 60%               |
| Q4      | 95% of ceiling               | 85%               |

Note: The minimum threshold is the floor that must not be breached below.
SKPDs that fail to meet the minimum threshold must submit a report and
acceleration plan to the Budget Team (TAPD) within 5 business days.

CHAPTER IV — CONTROL REQUIREMENTS AND SANCTIONS

Article 7 — Monitoring Mechanism

Budget realisation monitoring is carried out by:
a. SKPD internal review: every 2 weeks by the SKPD Head
b. BPKD: monthly, with report to the Regional Secretary
c. Regional Inspectorate: quarterly budget performance audit
d. Budget Team (TAPD): comprehensive evaluation per quarter

Article 8 — Sanctions for Low Absorption

SKPDs with low budget realisation face progressive sanctions:

Absorption below the Q3 minimum threshold (below 60% of ceiling):
- SKPD Head summoned by the Regional Secretary
- Obligation to submit a budget acceleration plan within 5 business days
- Restriction on submitting supplementary budget requests for the following year

End-of-year absorption below 75% of ceiling (penalty threshold exceeded):
- Mandatory return of remaining budget to the regional treasury
- Budget ceiling for the following year reduced by 2x the shortfall from the minimum
  Example: absorption 65% (10% below the 75% floor) → next year's ceiling cut by 20%
- SKPD Head performance evaluation by the Mayor
- Inspectorate recommendation for further review if irregularities are suspected

End-of-year absorption below 60% of ceiling (very low absorption):
- All of the above sanctions apply
- Special audit by the Inspectorate within 30 days after fiscal year close
- Report to the Supreme Audit Agency (BPK) and Regional House of Representatives (DPRD)
- SKPD institutional review (potential merger or restructuring)

Article 9 — Efficiency and Savings Recognition

SKPDs that execute programmes with 90–100% absorption and achieve performance
targets (outputs and outcomes) receive:
- Priority consideration in the following year's budget planning
- Additional civil servant performance incentive as permitted by regulations
- Mayor's award for the best-performing budget unit

Article 10 — Financial Reporting Requirements

Mandatory financial reports produced by each SKPD:
- Budget Realisation Report (LRA): monthly, no later than the 10th of the following month
- Operations Report (LO): quarterly
- Cash Flow Statement (LAK): monthly for SKPDs managing collection/disbursement accounts
- Balance Sheet: semi-annual
- Notes to Financial Statements (CaLK): annual
