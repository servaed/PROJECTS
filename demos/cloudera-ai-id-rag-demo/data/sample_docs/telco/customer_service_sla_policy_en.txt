CUSTOMER SERVICE POLICY AND SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)
Reference: TLC/CS/POL/001/2026-EN | Revision: 3.2 | Effective: 1 January 2026
Issued by: Board of Directors, PT Nusantara Telekomunikasi Tbk

CHAPTER I — GENERAL PROVISIONS

Article 1 — Definitions

1.1 Service Level Agreement (SLA) is a written commitment defining the minimum quality
    standards the company guarantees to its customers.
1.2 Churn is the condition in which a customer actively terminates a subscription or
    fails to renew an expiring contract.
1.3 Churn Risk Score is a predictive value (scale 0–100) indicating the probability that
    a customer will churn within the next 90 days:
    - Score 0–30   : Low risk
    - Score 31–69  : Medium risk (monthly monitoring)
    - Score 70–100 : High risk (active intervention required within 7 business days)
1.4 Net Promoter Score (NPS): a measure of customer loyalty on a scale of -100 to +100.
1.5 First Call Resolution (FCR): the percentage of complaints resolved on first contact.

CHAPTER II — NETWORK QUALITY STANDARDS

Article 2 — Network Availability

Guaranteed minimum network availability:
- 4G LTE : minimum 99.5% uptime per month (maximum downtime 3.6 hours/month)
- 5G     : minimum 99.7% uptime per month (maximum downtime 2.2 hours/month)
- 3G     : minimum 98.0% uptime per month

Network utilisation thresholds:
- Normal      : utilisation 0–70% of capacity
- Caution     : utilisation 70–85% (intensive monitoring, accelerated expansion planning)
- Critical    : utilisation 85–95% (restriction on adding new subscribers)
- Overcapacity: utilisation above 95% (network emergency, mandatory backup activation)

Article 3 — Minimum Data Speed Guarantees

Minimum download speeds guaranteed under the SLA:
- Starter Plan (5 GB)   : minimum 5 Mbps average
- Basic Plan (20 GB)    : minimum 10 Mbps average
- Standard Plan (50 GB) : minimum 20 Mbps average
- Premium Plan (100 GB) : minimum 30 Mbps average
- Unlimited Plan        : minimum 20 Mbps (no throttle during FUP)
- Enterprise Plan       : per individual contract, minimum 50 Mbps

CHAPTER III — CUSTOMER SERVICE STANDARDS

Article 4 — Complaint Handling Time

Ticket resolution times by severity:
- P1 Critical (total service outage, more than 500 customers affected)  : 4 hours
- P2 High (significant degradation, 100–500 customers affected)          : 8 hours
- P3 Medium (partial disruption, fewer than 100 customers)               : 24 hours
- P4 Low (enquiries, non-critical requests)                              : 3 business days
- P5 Informational (suggestions, general feedback)                       : 5 business days

First response time by channel:
- 24/7 critical fault hotline : immediate (within 30 seconds)
- 24/7 general enquiry hotline: under 2 minutes
- App live chat               : under 5 minutes
- Official email              : under 4 business hours
- GraPARI walk-in centre      : under 15 minutes queue time
- Social media                : under 1 hour

Article 5 — Customer Satisfaction Targets

- Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT): minimum 80% satisfied or very satisfied
- Net Promoter Score (NPS) target for 2026: minimum +35 points
- First Call Resolution (FCR):
  * Prepaid customers    : minimum 75%
  * Postpaid customers   : minimum 80%
  * Corporate customers  : minimum 85%

CHAPTER IV — CUSTOMER RETENTION PROGRAMME

Article 6 — Retention Programme Eligibility

Active retention offers are extended to customers who meet all of the following criteria:
a. Continuous subscription of at least 12 months
b. No outstanding payment in the last 6 months
c. Churn risk score of at least 70 (high risk), OR
   contract expiring within 60 days with a score above 50

Customers with scores below 70 do not qualify for the active retention programme.

Article 7 — Intervention by Churn Risk Score

- Score 70–79  : Renewal discount offer of 10–15%
- Score 80–89  : Free plan upgrade plus 15–20% discount offer
- Score 90–100 : Special offer (25–30% discount + bonus data quota + loyalty gift)

Article 8 — Contract Renewal Discounts

Conditions to qualify for a renewal discount:
a. Postpaid customer with continuous subscription of at least 12 months
b. Active account with no outstanding balance
c. Commitment to renew for at least 12 additional months
d. Discount applies to the monthly subscription charge, not to device costs

Discount tiers based on cumulative subscription duration:
- 12–23 months    : 10% off the monthly subscription rate
- 24–35 months    : 15% off the monthly subscription rate
- 36–47 months    : 20% off the monthly subscription rate
- 48 months and above: 25% off the monthly subscription rate

Article 9 — 2026 Churn Rate Targets

Monthly churn rate targets that must not be exceeded:
- Prepaid customers   : maximum 4.5% per month
- Postpaid customers  : maximum 1.8% per month
- Corporate customers : maximum 0.8% per month
- Total churn rate    : maximum 3.2% per month (weighted average)

CHAPTER V — SLA BREACH COMPENSATION

Article 10 — Network Outage Compensation

If the company fails to meet its committed SLA availability, customers are entitled
to automatic compensation:
- Downtime 1–4 hours     : 500 MB data credit
- Downtime 4–12 hours    : 2 GB data credit + 10% bill discount
- Downtime 12–24 hours   : 5 GB data credit + 25% bill discount
- Downtime more than 24 hours: 10 GB data credit + 50% discount + 1 free month extension

Article 11 — Compensation for Delayed Complaint Handling

- P1 target missed : IDR 50,000 credit to customer's account
- P2 target missed : IDR 25,000 credit to customer's account
- P3 target missed : IDR 10,000 credit to customer's account

CHAPTER VI — MONITORING AND REPORTING

Article 12 — Monthly KPIs

The Customer Experience Division must submit a monthly report to the Board covering:
- Churn rate per segment and comparison with targets
- Churn risk score distribution across the active portfolio
- Monthly CSAT, NPS, and Customer Effort Score (CES)
- SLA achievement rate per complaint category
- FCR per service channel

Article 13 — Escalation

If the churn rate exceeds limits or the SLA achievement rate falls below 90%
for two consecutive months, the Head of Customer Experience must submit a
corrective action plan to the Board within 5 business days.
