NETWORK OPERATIONS GUIDE AND SPECTRUM FREQUENCY REGULATION
Reference: TLC/NET/OPS/002/2026-EN | Revision: 2.1 | Effective: 1 January 2026
Issued by: Technology Director, PT Nusantara Telekomunikasi Tbk

CHAPTER I — SPECTRUM ALLOCATION AND LICENCES

Article 1 — Held Spectrum Allocation

PT Nusantara Telekomunikasi Tbk manages the following spectrum frequencies under
licences issued by the Ministry of Communication and Information Technology (Kominfo):

4G LTE Frequencies:
- 1800 MHz (Band 3)  : 20 MHz FDD, nationwide coverage
- 2100 MHz (Band 1)  : 15 MHz FDD, 32 major cities
- 2300 MHz (Band 40) : 30 MHz TDD, metropolitan areas

5G Frequencies:
- 3500 MHz (Band n78): 100 MHz TDD, Phase 1: 10 cities (2024–2026)
- 26 GHz (mmWave)    : 400 MHz, pilot hotspots in Jakarta and Surabaya

Legacy Frequencies:
- 900 MHz (Band 8)   : 10 MHz FDD, rural and remote coverage
- 2100 MHz (3G/UMTS) : 5 MHz FDD, scheduled sunset in 2027

Article 2 — Coverage Obligations

Pursuant to Kominfo licences and the Universal Service Obligation (USO) agreement:
- 4G coverage across all district/city capitals: 100% by end-2025 (ACHIEVED)
- 4G coverage in sub-districts with populations above 10,000: 95% by end-2026
- 3G coverage across all land territory: 98% (achieved as of 2023)
- 5G coverage in 50 major cities: 60% by end-2028

CHAPTER II — TECHNICAL NETWORK STANDARDS

Article 3 — BTS Capacity and Utilisation

Established network capacity standards:
- 4G/LTE standard BTS capacity: 50–150 Mbps (downlink) depending on configuration
- 5G NR BTS capacity: 500–2,000 Mbps (downlink) depending on carrier aggregation
- Maximum simultaneous active users per BTS: 200 (to maintain QoS)

Utilisation thresholds requiring action:
- Utilisation 70–80% : Accelerate capacity planning, coordinate with regional team
- Utilisation 80–85% : Mandatory frequency and network parameter optimisation
- Utilisation 85–90% : Escalate to central NOC, procure additional BTS units
- Utilisation above 90%: Critical status, restrict new SIM activations in affected area

Current network utilisation as of March 2026 (priority monitoring areas):
- Bali (Denpasar and surroundings)   : 90.1% — CRITICAL STATUS, active escalation
- East Java (Greater Surabaya)       : 85.6% — Critical, BTS expansion planned
- West Java (Greater Bandung)        : 71.2% — Caution, intensive monitoring
- Jakarta                             : 78.3% — Caution, carrier aggregation active
- Central Java (Semarang)            : 68.9% — Normal
- North Sumatra (Medan)              : 55.0% — Normal, adequate capacity
- South Sulawesi (Makassar)          : 48.2% — Normal
- East Kalimantan (Balikpapan)       : 42.7% — Normal

Article 4 — Network Fault Management

Fault classification and handling procedures:
- Level 1 (Minor): fewer than 10% of BTS in one area affected
  Handling: local team, target resolution 4 hours
- Level 2 (Major): 10–30% of BTS affected or critical area impacted
  Handling: regional team + central NOC, target resolution 8 hours
- Level 3 (Catastrophic): more than 30% of BTS affected or backbone down
  Handling: crisis team + Board of Directors, target resolution 24 hours + report to Kominfo

Article 5 — Planned Network Maintenance

Scheduled maintenance windows:
- Routine BTS maintenance: maximum 2 hours per month per BTS, between 02:00–05:00 WIB
- Network software upgrade: scheduled on weekends, maximum 4 hours
- Customer notification: at least 72 hours before any service-affecting maintenance

CHAPTER III — 5G EXPANSION ROADMAP

Article 6 — 5G Roadmap 2024–2028

Phase 1 (2024–2025) — COMPLETED:
- 5G deployment in Jakarta, Surabaya, Bandung, Medan, Makassar
- 1,250 active 5G base stations with total capacity of 450 Gbps
- Population coverage: 28% of the national population

Phase 2 (2026–2027) — IN PROGRESS:
- Expansion to 30 tier-2 cities: Semarang, Yogyakarta, Palembang, Balikpapan, etc.
- Target: 3,000 additional 5G base stations
- Investment target: IDR 8.5 trillion

Phase 3 (2028) — PLANNED:
- Expansion to 50+ cities and priority industrial zones
- Integration with LEO satellite networks for remote areas
- Target 5G population coverage: 60% of the national population

Article 7 — 5G Quality of Service Requirements

Minimum 5G service standards to be maintained:
- Peak download speed        : minimum 1 Gbps in urban areas
- Peak upload speed          : minimum 100 Mbps
- End-to-end latency         : maximum 10 ms (ultra-low latency slice)
- Connection density         : minimum 1 million devices per square kilometre
- 5G network availability    : minimum 99.7% monthly uptime

CHAPTER IV — NETWORK SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE

Article 8 — Infrastructure Security

Mandatory security standards for all network infrastructure:
- End-to-end encryption for all voice and data communications (3GPP standard)
- 24/7 cyber-security monitoring by the Security Operations Center (SOC)
- Network core penetration testing: minimum twice per year by an independent third party
- Backup power systems: minimum 8-hour UPS plus generator at every critical site

Article 9 — Regulatory Compliance

Mandatory reports to Kominfo and BRTI:
- Monthly Quality of Service (QoS) report: no later than the 15th of the following month
- Significant network fault report: within 2 hours of the incident
- Annual spectrum usage report: by 31 January each year

Penalties for spectrum regulation violations:
- Unlicensed frequency use: IDR 500 million – IDR 2 billion per case
- Late QoS report: IDR 100 million per delayed report
- Coverage obligation non-compliance: IDR 1 billion per quarter per region
