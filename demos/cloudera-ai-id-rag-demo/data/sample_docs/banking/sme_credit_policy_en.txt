SME CREDIT POLICY — MICRO, SMALL, AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES
Reference: KRD/POL/001/2026-EN | Revision: 4.0 | Effective: 1 January 2026
Issued by: Board of Directors, PT Bank Nusantara Tbk

CHAPTER I — GENERAL PROVISIONS

Article 1 — Definitions

1.1 SME Credit refers to financing facilities extended to micro, small, and medium enterprise
    operators in accordance with Law No. 20 of 2008 on MSMEs:
    - Micro Enterprise: maximum assets IDR 50 million, annual turnover up to IDR 300 million
    - Small Enterprise: assets IDR 50 million to IDR 500 million, turnover IDR 300 million to IDR 2.5 billion
    - Medium Enterprise: assets IDR 500 million to IDR 10 billion, turnover IDR 2.5 billion to IDR 50 billion

1.2 Credit Quality refers to the OJK classification under POJK No. 40/POJK.03/2019:
    - Performing (Lancar): on-time payment, no arrears
    - Special Mention (Dalam Perhatian Khusus/DPK): arrears 1–90 days
    - Substandard (Kurang Lancar): arrears 91–120 days
    - Doubtful (Diragukan): arrears 121–180 days
    - Loss (Macet): arrears more than 180 days

1.3 Non-Performing Loan (NPL) means credits classified as Substandard, Doubtful, or Loss.
1.4 Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR): ratio of operating cash flow to total debt obligations.

Article 2 — Objectives

a. Expand financial inclusion for SME operators across Indonesia
b. Support economic growth through measured and prudent credit expansion
c. Maintain portfolio quality in line with OJK standards
d. Achieve a 15% YoY growth target for SME credit in 2026
e. Keep the gross NPL ratio for SME credit below 3.0% on a consolidated basis

CHAPTER II — ELIGIBILITY AND DOCUMENTATION

Article 3 — Borrower Requirements

Applicants must satisfy all of the following requirements:
1. Indonesian citizen (WNI) aged at least 21 or legally married
2. Active business operating continuously for at least 2 years
3. Not listed on Bank Indonesia's Blacklist (DHBI)
4. No loan default at any financial institution in the past 24 months
5. Internal credit score of at least 600 on a 300–900 scale
6. DSCR of at least 1.2x

Article 4 — Restricted Sectors

The bank does not finance: gambling, high-alcohol beverages without a permit, illegal weapons
trading, environmentally destructive businesses, or businesses violating applicable laws.

Article 5 — Required Documentation

Identity Documents: National ID (KTP), Tax ID (NPWP — mandatory for limits above IDR 50 million),
Family Registration Card (KK), Marriage Certificate.

Business Documents:
- Business Identification Number (NIB) or valid business license (SIUP)
- Articles of incorporation and all amendments (for PT/CV/Firma entities)
- Business domicile certificate from the local kelurahan
- Photographs of business premises from at least 3 angles

Financial Documents:
- Two years of financial statements (income statement and balance sheet)
- Six months of bank statements from all active accounts
- Annual income tax returns for the past two years (mandatory for limits above IDR 200 million)

Collateral Documents (mandatory for small and medium segments):
- Land certificate (SHM or HGB) with building permit (IMB)
- Vehicle ownership certificate (BPKB) and registration (STNK)
- OJK-accredited independent appraisal report (for limits above IDR 1 billion)

CHAPTER III — CREDIT LIMITS, TENOR, AND INTEREST RATES

Article 6 — Credit Limit Tiers

| Segment | Minimum Limit    | Maximum Limit    | Collateral Requirement     |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| Micro   | IDR 5 million   | IDR 500 million | Not required (up to 50M)   |
| Small   | IDR 500 million | IDR 5 billion   | Required                   |
| Medium  | IDR 5 billion   | IDR 50 billion  | Required + Appraisal       |

Article 7 — Loan Tenor

| Segment | Working Capital  | Investment       |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Micro   | Max. 12 months  | Max. 36 months  |
| Small   | Max. 24 months  | Max. 60 months  |
| Medium  | Max. 36 months  | Max. 84 months  |

Article 8 — Interest Rates

- Micro KUR (government-subsidised): 6% per annum effective
- Micro non-KUR: 12–15% per annum effective
- Small segment: 9–13% per annum effective
- Medium segment: 8–11% per annum effective

CHAPTER IV — APPROVAL PROCESS AND EXPANSION TARGETS

Article 9 — Approval Authority

- Up to IDR 500 million: Branch Head
- IDR 500 million – IDR 5 billion: Regional Credit Committee
- Above IDR 5 billion: Central Credit Committee

Standard processing times:
- Micro segment (automated scoring): 1 business day
- Small segment: 7 business days from complete documentation
- Medium segment: 14 business days from complete documentation

Article 10 — 2026 Portfolio Expansion Targets

The bank sets a national SME credit growth target of 15% YoY against the
end-December 2025 outstanding position (national total: IDR 485 trillion).

Regional growth targets:
- DKI Jakarta         : +18% YoY (end-2025 outstanding: IDR 92.4 trillion)
- West Java           : +16% YoY (end-2025 outstanding: IDR 68.1 trillion)
- East Java           : +15% YoY (end-2025 outstanding: IDR 71.3 trillion)
- Central Java        : +12% YoY (end-2025 outstanding: IDR 54.8 trillion)
- North Sumatra       : +20% YoY (end-2025 outstanding: IDR 28.5 trillion)
- South Sulawesi      : +25% YoY (end-2025 outstanding: IDR 15.2 trillion)
- Bali & Nusa Tenggara: +22% YoY (end-2025 outstanding: IDR 12.1 trillion)

End-2026 outstanding targets by segment:
- Micro  : IDR 167 trillion (from IDR 145 trillion)
- Small  : IDR 251 trillion (from IDR 218 trillion)
- Medium : IDR 140 trillion (from IDR 122 trillion)

CHAPTER V — QUALITY MONITORING AND RESTRUCTURING

Article 11 — Portfolio Quality Standards

Board-approved tolerances for 2026:
- Gross NPL         : maximum 3.0% of total SME outstanding
- Net NPL           : maximum 1.5% (after loan-loss provisions)
- Special Mention ratio: maximum 8.0% of total SME outstanding
- Provision coverage: minimum 100% for all NPL credits

Article 12 — Region Classification by Credit Quality

Regions are classified quarterly based on gross NPL:
- Green  (NPL < 1.5%)  : full expansion with no restrictions
- Yellow (NPL 1.5%–3.0%): controlled expansion under intensive oversight
- Red    (NPL > 3.0%)  : moratorium on new credit, focus on recovery

Status as of December 2025:
- DKI Jakarta  : NPL 1.8% — Yellow (controlled expansion)
- Surabaya     : NPL 1.2% — Green (full expansion permitted)
- Bandung      : NPL 2.4% — Yellow (controlled expansion)
- Medan        : NPL 1.0% — Green (full expansion permitted)
- Makassar     : NPL 0.9% — Green (full expansion permitted)

Article 13 — Restructuring Eligibility Conditions

A loan may be restructured if at least one of the following conditions is met:
a. Credit quality is Special Mention or worse (arrears of at least 30 days)
b. Borrower is affected by force majeure: natural disaster, pandemic, or government policy
   directly impacting the business
c. Turnover has declined more than 30% due to documented external economic conditions
d. Loss of a key customer accounting for more than 40% of revenue
e. Supply chain disruption affecting business continuity for at least 3 months

Restructuring schemes by credit quality:
- Special Mention : term extension or temporary interest rate reduction
- Substandard     : installment rescheduling or grace period of 3–6 months
- Doubtful        : combination of rescheduling and interest rate reduction
- Loss            : transferred directly to Recovery Management Unit; not eligible for restructuring

Article 14 — Reporting

SME portfolio quality reports are submitted to:
- Risk Management Committee: monthly
- Board of Commissioners: quarterly
- OJK: as required under SLIK and LKPBU regulations

This policy is reviewed at least annually or whenever a significant OJK regulatory change occurs.
