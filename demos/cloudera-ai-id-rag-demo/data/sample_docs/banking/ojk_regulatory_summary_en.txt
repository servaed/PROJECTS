OJK REGULATORY SUMMARY — SME CREDIT OPERATIONS
Reference: REG/OJK/003/2026-EN | Effective: 1 January 2026
Prepared by: Legal and Compliance Division, PT Bank Nusantara Tbk

PART I — CORE BANKING CREDIT REGULATIONS

1. POJK No. 40/POJK.03/2019 — Assessment of Commercial Bank Asset Quality

Key provisions summary:

1.1 Credit quality classifications and minimum provisions:
    - Performing (Lancar)          : minimum 1% provision of outstanding
    - Special Mention (DPK)        : minimum 5% provision of outstanding
    - Substandard (Kurang Lancar)  : minimum 15% provision of outstanding
    - Doubtful (Diragukan)         : minimum 50% provision of outstanding
    - Loss (Macet)                 : minimum 100% provision of outstanding

1.2 Write-off of non-performing loans:
    Banks must write off loans classified as Loss that have been in arrears for more
    than 270 days with no realistic prospect of recovery. Write-off does not extinguish
    the bank's right to pursue collection from the borrower.

1.3 Loan restructuring:
    A restructured loan may be reclassified as Performing (Lancar) if the borrower
    makes three consecutive on-time payments after restructuring.

2. POJK No. 11/POJK.03/2020 — National Economic Stimulus (Extended through 2025)

2.1 Simplified restructuring for pandemic-affected borrowers:
    - Credit quality may be immediately reclassified as Performing after restructuring
    - No new collectibility analysis required for limits up to IDR 10 billion

2.2 Current status: normalisation provisions came into full effect on 1 January 2026.
    All credits previously under stimulus are again subject to POJK 40/2019.

3. POJK No. 6/POJK.03/2022 — Legal Lending Limit (LLL / BMPK)

3.1 LLL for related parties: maximum 10% of bank capital
3.2 LLL for non-related parties: maximum 25% of bank capital
3.3 LLL for state-owned and regional enterprises: maximum 30% of bank capital

PART II — LIQUIDITY AND CAPITAL REGULATIONS

4. POJK No. 12/POJK.03/2021 — Minimum Capital Adequacy Requirements

4.1 Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) minimums:
    - Bank category 1 (KBMI 1): minimum CAR 8%
    - Bank category 2 (KBMI 2): minimum CAR 9%
    - Bank category 3 (KBMI 3): minimum CAR 10%
    - Bank category 4 (KBMI 4): minimum CAR 14%

4.2 PT Bank Nusantara Tbk is in KBMI 3 with a minimum CAR requirement of 10%.
    CAR position as of December 2025: 18.4% (above regulatory threshold).

5. POJK No. 50/POJK.03/2017 — Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)

5.1 Minimum LCR for banks with assets above IDR 200 trillion: 100%
5.2 Minimum Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR): 100%
5.3 Bank Nusantara LCR position as of December 2025: 142% (above minimum)

PART III — SME-SPECIFIC CREDIT REGULATIONS

6. Government Regulation No. 7 of 2021 — MSME Facilitation, Protection, and Empowerment

6.1 State-owned banks and regional development banks must allocate at least 30% of their
    credit portfolio to the SME segment (progressive obligation through 2024).
6.2 Private national commercial banks: recommended minimum 20% for SME credit.
6.3 PT Bank Nusantara Tbk: current SME credit share is 24.3% of total portfolio,
    with a target of 27% by end-2026.

7. KUR People's Business Credit Programme 2026

7.1 KUR disbursement quota for Bank Nusantara 2026: IDR 12.5 trillion
7.2 Allocation by segment:
    - Micro KUR (up to IDR 100 million): IDR 7.5 trillion
    - Small KUR (IDR 100 million – IDR 500 million): IDR 4.0 trillion
    - Special KUR (agriculture, fisheries, etc.): IDR 1.0 trillion
7.3 KUR interest rate: 6% per annum (effective), with government interest subsidy
7.4 Collateral for Micro KUR up to IDR 100 million: not required

8. NPL Requirements and SLIK Reporting

8.1 Banks must report borrower data to OJK's Financial Information Services System (SLIK):
    - Every borrower with a credit limit above IDR 50 million
    - Frequency: monthly, no later than the 14th of the following month

8.2 NPL thresholds monitored by OJK:
    - Warning level: Gross NPL > 2.5%
    - Intensive supervision: Gross NPL > 4.0%
    - Special measures (pengawasan khusus): Gross NPL > 5.0%

8.3 Bank Nusantara is required to maintain total gross NPL below 2.5% in line with its
    internal target and OJK's warning level threshold.

PART IV — CONSUMER PROTECTION AND TRANSPARENCY

9. POJK No. 6/POJK.07/2022 — Consumer and Public Protection in the Financial Services Sector

9.1 Bank obligations regarding SME borrower protection:
    - Fee transparency: all costs must be stated in the credit agreement
    - Hidden charges not disclosed to prospective borrowers are prohibited
    - Borrowers have the right to receive a copy of the credit agreement at no extra cost
    - Unethical debt collection practices (intimidation, threats, etc.) are prohibited

9.2 Borrower complaint mechanism:
    - Banks must maintain an active consumer complaint unit
    - Resolution deadline: 20 business days for written complaints
    - Escalation to OJK if not resolved within the deadline

10. Reference Interest Rate Requirements

10.1 Since 2021, banks are not required to use a specific reference rate for loan pricing,
     but must disclose:
     - Prime lending rate (SBDK) per segment
     - Components making up the SBDK (cost of funds, overhead, profit margin)

10.2 Bank Nusantara prime lending rates in effect (January 2026):
     - Corporate Credit   : 9.25% per annum
     - Retail Credit      : 10.75% per annum
     - SME Credit         : 11.50% per annum (non-KUR)
     - Consumer Credit    : 12.00% per annum
