KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC) AND ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) PROCEDURES
Reference: KYC/PROC/002/2026-EN | Revision: 5.1 | Effective: 1 January 2026
Issued by: Compliance Director, PT Bank Nusantara Tbk

CHAPTER I — LEGAL BASIS AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

Article 1 — Legal Basis

1.1 Law No. 8 of 2010 on Prevention and Eradication of Money Laundering (TPPU)
1.2 POJK No. 12/POJK.01/2017 on Implementation of AML and Counter-Terrorism Financing
    Programmes in the Financial Services Sector
1.3 Bank Indonesia Regulation No. 19/10/PBI/2017 on Anti-Money Laundering Implementation
1.4 FATF Recommendations 2012 (40 international AML standards)

Article 2 — Definitions

2.1 Customer Due Diligence (CDD): the process of identifying, verifying, and monitoring customers.
2.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD): strengthened CDD procedures for high-risk customers.
2.3 Simplified Due Diligence (SDD): simplified CDD procedures for low-risk customers.
2.4 Politically Exposed Person (PEP): an individual who holds or has held a prominent public
    position at the national or international level, including immediate family and close associates.
2.5 Beneficial Owner (BO): a natural person who directly or indirectly owns or controls the
    customer, and/or who is the ultimate beneficiary of a transaction.
2.6 Suspicious Transaction Report (STR): a report of suspicious financial transactions submitted
    to the Financial Intelligence Unit (PPATK).

CHAPTER II — CUSTOMER RISK CLASSIFICATION

Article 3 — Risk Tiers

The bank classifies customer risk into three tiers:

Low Risk:
- Civil servants (non-officials) with regular salary
- Retirees with fixed income from a pension fund
- Students with verified parental funding
- Micro/small businesses with annual turnover below IDR 300 million
- Applicable procedure: SDD (Simplified Due Diligence)

Medium Risk:
- Self-employed individuals with annual turnover IDR 300 million – IDR 10 billion
- Independent professionals (doctors, lawyers, accountants, consultants)
- Domestic corporations not classified as high-risk
- Applicable procedure: standard CDD

High Risk — EDD mandatory:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their family members/close associates
- Customers from countries on the FATF non-cooperative countries list
- Money service businesses (money changers, remittance operators)
- Dealers in luxury goods (gold, gems, premium vehicles)
- Non-profit organisations with significant cross-border transactions
- Customers who refuse to provide information requested by the bank

CHAPTER III — STANDARD CDD PROCEDURES

Article 4 — Individual Customer Identification

Mandatory identification documents:
a. National ID card (KTP Elektronik) — original and photocopy, required for all Indonesian citizens
b. Valid passport for foreign nationals
c. KITAS/KITAP for foreign nationals residing in Indonesia
d. Tax ID (NPWP) — mandatory for transactions above IDR 100 million or business account opening

Information that must be obtained and verified:
- Full name as shown on official identification
- Place and date of birth
- Nationality and residential status
- Home address and mailing address
- Tax identification number (NPWP)
- Occupation and employer name and address
- Purpose of account opening and expected source of funds
- Estimated monthly transaction frequency and value
- Confirmation of whether the customer is a PEP

Article 5 — Corporate Customer Identification

For corporate customers, the bank must obtain:

Corporate documents:
- Articles of incorporation and all amendments ratified by the Ministry of Law (for PT)
- Business license (SIUP/TDP) or Business Identification Number (NIB)
- Corporate Tax ID (NPWP Badan)
- Domicile certificate or evidence of business premises
- Latest financial statements (audited for companies with turnover above IDR 50 billion)

Beneficial ownership information:
- Identity of all shareholders/cooperative members holding more than 25% (KTP/Passport copy)
- Confirmation of whether any beneficial owner is a PEP
- Ownership structure chart down to the ultimate beneficial owner (UBO)

Article 6 — Document Verification

Accepted verification methods:
a. In-person verification at a branch office — standard for all segments
b. Video call with a trained bank officer — for customers beyond branch reach
c. e-KYC via BankNusa Mobile app — for low-risk customers, limits up to IDR 200 million
d. OJK-accredited third party — with cooperation agreement and bank oversight

Article 7 — Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

For high-risk customers, the bank must carry out:
a. Mandatory in-person verification by a bank official (at least section head level)
b. Comprehensive source of wealth identification
c. Senior management approval before the business relationship commences
d. Enhanced transaction monitoring: monthly report to the Compliance Officer
e. Customer profile review every 6 months (standard CDD customers: every 12 months)

CHAPTER IV — ONGOING TRANSACTION MONITORING

Article 8 — Reporting Thresholds

Transactions that must be reported to PPATK:

Cash Transaction Reports (CTR):
- Cash transactions of IDR 500 million or more in a single day
- Multiple cash transactions that appear related with a cumulative total exceeding IDR 500 million/day

Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR):
- Transactions inconsistent with the customer's profile without a reasonable explanation
- Transactions suspected to be proceeds of a criminal offence
- Customers who refuse to provide requested information or provide false information
- Transfers to/from countries on the FATF non-cooperative countries list

Cross-Border Wire Transfer Reports:
- Any outward wire transfer of IDR 1 billion or more

Article 9 — Account Freezing and Closure

The bank has the right to freeze accounts and refuse transactions when:
a. The customer is on a list of suspected terrorists or terrorism financiers
b. The customer is subject to international sanctions (OFAC, UN Security Council)
c. There is a freeze order from PPATK or a competent law enforcement authority
d. The customer is proven to have provided false information during KYC

CHAPTER V — DATA REFRESH AND PERIODIC REVIEW

Article 10 — KYC Review Schedule

| Risk Category | Review Frequency | Additional Review Trigger         |
|--------------|-----------------|----------------------------------|
| Low          | Every 24 months | Transaction profile change >50%  |
| Medium       | Every 12 months | Transaction profile change >30%  |
| High         | Every 6 months  | Any anomalous transaction        |

Article 11 — Consequences of Non-Compliance

The bank may apply the following measures for uncooperative customers:
a. Partial transaction restriction (partial freeze) until data is updated
b. Account closure with 30 days' written notice
c. Report to PPATK as a suspicious transaction
d. Denial of all bank products and services

CHAPTER VI — TRAINING AND INTERNAL COMPLIANCE

Article 12 — Employee Training Requirements

All bank employees who interact with customers must complete:
- Mandatory AML/CFT training: minimum 8 hours per year
- Internal KYC certification: mandatory before front-office assignment
- Regulatory comprehension test: every 2 years or after significant regulatory changes

Article 13 — Internal Reporting

The Compliance Officer must submit reports to the Compliance Director:
- Monthly CTR and STR summary
- Semi-annual KYC implementation progress report
- Annual KYC audit results report
